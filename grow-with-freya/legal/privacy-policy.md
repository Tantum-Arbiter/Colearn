# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: January 12, 2026**

## 1. Introduction

Grow with Freya ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting the privacy of children and families who use our educational mobile application. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect information when you use the Grow with Freya app ("App").

**IMPORTANT FOR PARENTS:** This App is designed for children ages 2-8. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and other applicable privacy laws. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children without parental consent.

## 2. Information We Collect

### 2.1 Information We Do NOT Collect
We are committed to minimal data collection. We do NOT collect:
- Children's names, photos, or personal identifiers
- Location data or GPS information
- Contact lists or social media information
- Biometric data
- Audio or video recordings of children

### 2.2 Information We May Collect
We may collect only the following limited information:

**Device Information:**
- Device type and operating system version
- App version and usage statistics
- Crash reports and error logs (anonymized, **opt-in only** - requires your explicit consent)

**Usage Data:**
- Which stories or activities are accessed
- Time spent in different app sections
- Progress through educational content (stored locally on device)

**Account Information (if applicable):**
- Parent/guardian email address (for account creation only)
- Subscription status and billing information (processed by app stores)

## 3. How We Use Information

We use collected information only to:
- Provide and improve our educational services
- Ensure the App functions properly on your device
- Analyze usage patterns to improve content (using anonymized data)
- Communicate with parents about account or subscription matters
- Comply with legal obligations

## 4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

### 4.1 We Do NOT Share Children's Information
We do not sell, rent, or share any information about children with third parties for marketing purposes.

### 4.2 Limited Sharing
We may share anonymized, aggregated data with:
- Service providers who help us operate the App (under strict confidentiality agreements)
- Analytics services to understand app performance (no personal information)

### 4.3 Legal Requirements
We may disclose information if required by law or to protect the safety of children or others.

## 5. Data Storage and Security

### 5.1 Local Storage
Most user progress and preferences are stored locally on your device and are not transmitted to our servers.

### 5.2 Security Measures
We implement appropriate security measures including:
- Encryption of data in transit and at rest
- Regular security audits and updates
- Limited access to any collected data
- Secure deletion of data when no longer needed

### 5.3 Data Retention
- Device and usage data: Retained only as long as necessary for app functionality
- Account data: Retained until account deletion or as required by law
- Children's data: Deleted immediately upon request or when no longer needed

## 6. Parental Rights and Controls

### 6.1 Parental Access
Parents have the right to:
- Review any information we have collected about their child
- Request deletion of their child's information
- Refuse to allow further collection of their child's information
- Contact us with questions about our privacy practices

### 6.2 How to Exercise Rights
To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@growwithfreya.com with:
- Your name and relationship to the child
- The child's information you're inquiring about
- Your specific request

### 6.3 Account Deletion
You may delete your account and all associated data at any time through the App settings or by contacting us.

## 7. Third-Party Services

### 7.1 App Stores
Downloads and purchases are processed through app stores (Apple App Store, Google Play Store), which have their own privacy policies.

### 7.2 Crash Reporting (Optional, Consent-Based)
We offer an **optional** crash reporting feature powered by Sentry (https://sentry.io). This feature is:

- **Opt-in only**: Crash reporting is disabled by default and requires explicit consent during onboarding
- **Revocable**: You can enable or disable crash reporting at any time in Settings → Privacy & Legal → Crash Reports
- **Anonymous**: Crash reports contain only technical information about what went wrong (error messages, stack traces, device type, OS version)
- **Privacy-focused**: We have disabled personally identifiable information (PII) collection. No IP addresses, user identifiers, or personal data are collected

**What crash reports contain:**
- Technical error details (what went wrong in the app)
- Device type and operating system version
- App version
- Timestamp of the crash

**What crash reports do NOT contain:**
- Your name, email, or any personal information
- Your child's information or activity
- Photos, audio, or any content from the app
- Location data or IP addresses
- Device identifiers that could identify you

For more information about Sentry's privacy practices, visit: https://sentry.io/privacy/

### 7.3 Analytics
We may use privacy-compliant analytics services that do not collect personal information about children.

### 7.4 No Advertising
Our App does not display third-party advertisements or collect data for advertising purposes.

## 8. International Users

If you are located outside the United States, please note that we may transfer and process your information in the United States, where our servers are located.

## 9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will:
- Notify parents of material changes via email or app notification
- Post the updated policy in the App
- Obtain new parental consent if required by law

## 10. COPPA Compliance

We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA):
- We do not collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent
- We do not share children's personal information with third parties
- Parents can review, delete, or refuse further collection of their child's information
- We do not condition a child's participation on providing more information than necessary
